My name is Victoria Brigitt and I am a current first year English major at the University of California, Riverside. I graduated from Sherman Oaks Center for Enriched Studies in June of 2019 with High Honors, received the California Seal of Biliteracy, and received the Golden Oak Award. I hope to graduate from UCR in the spring of 2023, attend law school the following fall, and after to become a family attorney.

I am very passionate about issues pertaining to child abuse and domestic violence, and hope to one day commit myself to helping those who suffer from such as a lawyer. At UCR, I am currently involved with the Global Brigades in the Legal/ Human Rights chapter. Global Brigades allows me to get involved in both local and global community service and relief projects through education and resource relief. I am relatively new to the organization, but am eager to further immerse myself in humanitarian work. I am very fortunate to have found Global Brigades because it allows me to learn more about legal rights and legal work, while volunteering to help communities in need. In addition to Global Brigades, I have been involved with another organization, but for a much longer time. I have played table tennis for 12 years and have been actively involved with the Sports and Art Education Foundation for 10 of those years. At SAEF, we promote table tennis as a source of therapy for Alzheimer's patients through the events and training sessions organized by the organization.

Currently studying as an English major, I am hoping to develop my reading, writing, reasoning, and thinking skills in preparation for the LSAT and Law School.
